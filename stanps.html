Rococo Stamps

The Valentine's Day Special Edition Rococo Vintage Stamps are a celebration of love, intrigue, and
historical art, inspired by Jean-Honoré Fragonard’s iconic Rococo painting, The
Swing (1767). The painting is a masterpiece of romantic complexity, depicting a playful love triangle: a young woman joyfully swinging, her clandestine lover hidden in the bushes gazing at her, and her oblivious older
husband standing on the right, pushing the swing. The composition captures flirtation, secrecy, and
the complexities of human relationships, making it the perfect theme for a Valentine's Day special.
The twist? The painting is divided into three parts, each featured on an individual stamp—one
showing the young lover, another the woman, and the third the husband. To reveal the full artwork,
buyers must collect all three stamps, making it an irresistible keepsake for art and romance enthusiasts. This innovative design serves a dual purpose: symbolizing love stories as complex as the painting
itself, and creating a collectible treasure. Marketing emphasizes the intrigue—impress your loved
one by sending all three stamps to convey your message or let recipients creatively interpret their
situation. Additionally, the campaign ensures higher sales since sending the complete story requires
purchasing all three stamps, turning love letters into a historical and artistic delight.
